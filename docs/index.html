ITERA

Every launch begins again.

A disposable-identity browser for Windows. Every launch is a brand-new user for the internet — and when you close it, a terminal erases every trace in front of you.

Download for Windows View on GitHub

Free & open source · Windows 10 / 11 · ~75 MB installer

Why ITERA

Built to forget you

No accounts, no history, no continuity. Every session is sealed off from the last.

Fresh identity each launch

A brand-new profile every time — zero cookies, cache, storage or login carried over.

Visible wipe on exit

A real terminal opens and runs every deletion command, so you watch your tracks burn.

Randomized fingerprint

A fresh, realistic user-agent is generated for every session to blunt fingerprinting.

Every site just works

A full Chromium engine under the hood — YouTube, search and the modern web all run.

Kill-session button

One click nukes the current identity and wipes everything — a panic button, any time.

Nothing phones home

No telemetry, no sync, no accounts. Open source — read exactly what it does.

The ritual

Launch. Browse. Burn.

Three steps, and you're no one again.

1

Launch

ITERA spins up a fresh, isolated profile and a randomized identity. Type a query and hit Begin.

2

Browse

Use the web normally. Nothing is shared with past or future sessions — you're a clean slate.

3

Burn

Close the window (or hit Kill session). A terminal opens and erases every trace on disk, visibly.

ITERA — Erasing your identity
   ITERA   -   Every launch begins again.
   ==================================================

   --- Cookies & login sessions ---
      > del /f /q "...\Network\Cookies"
   --- Caches (disk, code, GPU, shader) ---
      > rmdir /s /q "...\Cache"
      > rmdir /s /q "...\GPUCache"
   --- Local storage, IndexedDB, service workers ---
      > rmdir /s /q "...\Local Storage"
      > rmdir /s /q "...\IndexedDB"
   --- Final sweep: the entire profile folder ---
      > rmdir /s /q "...\itera"

   [ DONE ]  Everything is permanently erased.
   You are no one again.
